GLUTE TATTOO DESIGN GUIDE
InkRoute AI Knowledge Base

==================================================
PURPOSE
==================================================

Design one isolated tattoo specifically intended for ONE SIDE OF THE GLUTE.

Never generate a human body, glute, hip, skin, underwear, or body mockup.

The artwork must function as a standalone tattoo design while naturally fitting the broad, rounded surface of one glute.

==================================================
PLACEMENT PRIORITY
==================================================

The glute is primarily a compact, rounded placement.

Prioritize:

• Broad rounded coverage
• Compact composition
• Strong central mass
• Soft outer curvature
• Balanced use of width and height
• Cohesive silhouette

The design should feel naturally contained within one rounded anatomical area.

Do not treat this placement like a thigh, back, ribs, or sleeve.

==================================================
ANATOMICAL ANALYSIS
==================================================

One glute provides a broad, convex and approximately rounded tattoo surface.

Unlike elongated placements, there is no strong vertical axis that needs to carry the composition through a long distance.

Width and height should remain relatively similar.

The artwork may extend slightly farther in one direction when required by the subject, but it should still read as a compact rounded composition.

Avoid strong vertical stretching.

Avoid excessive horizontal stretching.

Avoid long narrow extensions.

==================================================
OVERALL FOOTPRINT
==================================================

The preferred overall footprint is:

• Near-circular
• Soft oval
• Rounded organic cluster
• Compact radial composition
• Broad rounded silhouette

The tattoo does NOT need to form a mathematically perfect circle.

The subject and tattoo style should determine the exact outer contour while remaining inside a generally rounded footprint.

The silhouette may contain natural irregularities such as petals, fur, flames, leaves, horns, wings, ornamental extensions, or other subject-specific forms.

These should support the rounded mass rather than turning the design into a long composition.

==================================================
ASPECT RATIO
==================================================

Preferred height-to-width relationship:

approximately 1 : 1

Typical acceptable range:

0.9 : 1
to
1.2 : 1

Minor variation is acceptable when naturally required by the requested subject.

Do not force an elongated 1.5 : 1 composition.

==================================================
COMPOSITION
==================================================

Use one clear dominant focal point or one tightly connected primary subject group.

Build secondary elements around the main subject to reinforce the rounded footprint.

Visual weight should remain concentrated near the central and middle areas.

Supporting elements may expand outward in several directions rather than traveling primarily upward or downward.

Avoid large empty gaps that divide the tattoo into separate pieces.

Avoid disconnected sticker-like elements.

==================================================
VISUAL FLOW
==================================================

Flow should move around, across, or slightly diagonally through the central mass.

Radial and curved movement works especially well.

The eye should circulate naturally through the composition instead of following a long sleeve-like path.

==================================================
SUITABLE SUBJECT BEHAVIOR
==================================================

Many subjects can work when adapted correctly.

Examples include:

✓ Flowers and peonies
✓ Animal heads
✓ Skull compositions
✓ Oni masks
✓ Butterflies and moths
✓ Snakes arranged through compact curves or coils
✓ Ornamental designs
✓ Mandala-inspired compositions
✓ Moons
✓ New School characters
✓ Traditional motifs
✓ Japanese masks or compact Japanese compositions
✓ Fine Line compositions

Large or naturally elongated subjects should be reorganized into a compact composition rather than stretched vertically.

==================================================
SUBJECT SCALING
==================================================

Do not place a small subject inside a large empty circular boundary.

Allow the primary subject to occupy substantial visual area.

Supporting elements should help complete the outer footprint.

For faces, masks, skulls, and animal heads, allow the focal subject to dominate the center.

For floral designs, build outward through overlapping flowers, leaves, and stems while preserving a compact mass.

For snakes, use controlled curves or coils instead of a long descending body.

==================================================
SILHOUETTE INTERPRETATION
==================================================

The anatomy defines a generally rounded available area.

It does NOT require every tattoo to have a perfectly circular outline.

Do not draw an artificial circle around the artwork.

Do not crop elements to manufacture a circular boundary.

Do not force every outer edge to follow identical curvature.

Instead, distribute the subject and supporting elements so their combined visual mass naturally forms a near-circular or softly oval footprint.

==================================================
DETAIL DISTRIBUTION
==================================================

Keep the highest detail and strongest contrast around the primary focal point.

Use medium detail in supporting elements.

Simplify minor outer elements where appropriate for tattoo readability.

Maintain clear negative-space separation between important overlapping forms.

Avoid uniform micro-detail across the entire composition.

==================================================
STYLE ADAPTATION
==================================================

Preserve the requested tattoo style.

Do not automatically make the tattoo floral, delicate, feminine, or ornamental simply because the placement is the glute.

A dark, aggressive, masculine, colorful, traditional, realistic, geometric, illustrative, or other requested style is valid if its composition is adapted to the rounded placement.

Placement controls the footprint.

Style controls the visual language.

Subject controls the exact silhouette.

==================================================
COMMON AI MISTAKES
==================================================

Never:

• Generate a body or glute
• Generate skin or a body mockup
• Create a thigh-like vertical composition
• Create a long sleeve composition
• Create a ribs-style flowing strip
• Create a wide lower-back composition
• Stretch the main subject vertically
• Force a perfect geometric circle
• Put the tattoo inside a circular frame unless requested
• Make every design floral or feminine
• Create a square or rectangular poster
• Use disconnected filler elements
• Crop the artwork

==================================================
OUTPUT REQUIREMENTS
==================================================

Generate only one isolated tattoo artwork.

Plain white or very light neutral background.

Entire tattoo fully visible.

Clear empty margin around the complete design.

No body.

No skin.

No mockup.

No text unless specifically requested.
