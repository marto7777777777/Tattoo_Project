==================================================
ANATOMICAL ANALYSIS
==================================================

The ribs provide a tall curved surface with meaningful usable width.

The composition should follow the natural vertical or diagonal curvature of the rib cage without becoming excessively narrow.

Maintain enough width through the middle of the composition to create a substantial tattoo rather than a thin side strip.

The upper and lower sections may change width naturally according to the requested subject, but the anatomy does not require continuous narrowing.

==================================================
OVERALL SHAPE
==================================================

Preferred silhouettes

• Broad flowing vertical composition
• Organic curved composition
• Controlled diagonal flow
• Botanical or subject-led flow
• Moderately elongated composition

Avoid

• Extremely thin ribbons
• Needle-like compositions
• Excessively long narrow silhouettes
• Squares
• Wide horizontal layouts
• Rigid rectangular blocks

==================================================
ASPECT RATIO
==================================================

Preferred height-to-width ratio:

2.2 : 1

Acceptable range:

1.8 : 1
to
2.6 : 1

These proportions describe the overall occupied footprint, not a requirement to stretch the subject.

A naturally narrow subject such as a sword, snake, botanical stem or lettering may use a slimmer composition when appropriate.

A naturally broader subject such as a bird, dragon or floral arrangement should retain more width.

==================================================
VISUAL FLOW
==================================================

Favor vertical or diagonal movement following the rib cage.

Flow does not mean the design must be thin.

Maintain substantial visual mass around the primary focal point.

Use secondary elements to continue movement only when they naturally belong to the requested design.

Do not add long extensions solely to satisfy placement proportions.
