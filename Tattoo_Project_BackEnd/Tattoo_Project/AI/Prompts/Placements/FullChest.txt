FULL CHEST TATTOO DESIGN GUIDE
InkRoute AI Knowledge Base

==================================================
PURPOSE
==================================================

Design one isolated tattoo specifically for the FULL CHEST.

Do NOT generate a torso, chest, skin or mockup.

The composition must be designed across BOTH pectorals and connected through the sternum region.

==================================================
ANATOMICAL FOOTPRINT
==================================================

Do NOT treat the full chest as a rectangle, oval or horizontal banner.

Think of the placement as TWO broad pectoral fields connected around a central sternum axis.

Each side has:

• a broad main pectoral mass
• a softly rounded lower contour
• an upper contour following the chest/clavicle
• an outer-upper contour that rises toward its shoulder

Together, both sides create a broad chest-shaped composition.

The outer left and right portions may rise toward the shoulders.

The lower contour should remain broad across both pectorals rather than converging into one central point.

==================================================
STERNUM RELATIONSHIP
==================================================

The sternum is the central anatomical anchor.

Important central subjects may occupy or cross this axis.

Supporting elements should then distribute visual weight across BOTH pectorals.

Do not stack the entire design vertically around the sternum.

Do not leave the pectoral areas empty while concentrating everything in the center.

==================================================
TWO NIPPLE-SAFE REGIONS
==================================================

Assume there are two nipple regions, one within each lower-central pectoral area, even though no body is shown.

The composition must remain visually complete if either region is left untouched or minimally tattooed.

Never make either nipple-safe region essential to:

• a face
• eye
• head
• central symbol
• critical anatomy
• major structural connection
• important outline
• text

Prefer interruptible supporting material around these areas:

• clouds
• wind
• waves
• foliage
• petals
• secondary feathers
• simple scales
• shadow
• texture
• negative space
• other non-critical forms

Do NOT visibly draw nipple circles or artificial holes.

The adaptation must be implicit in the composition.

==================================================
PROPORTIONS AND FLOW
==================================================

The full composition must be substantially wider than tall.

Movement should establish a strong relationship with the sternum and then spread across both pectorals.

Outer portions may rise naturally toward both shoulders.

The lower composition should follow broad rounded pectoral flow.

Do NOT progressively narrow the entire design downward.

==================================================
BALANCE
==================================================

Maintain balanced visual weight across left and right pectorals.

Perfect mirror symmetry is NOT required.

Symmetrical subjects may use symmetry.

Dynamic subjects may use controlled asymmetry while still occupying both sides convincingly.

==================================================
SILHOUETTE
==================================================

The full outer contour should feel anatomically chest-shaped:

• broad through both pectorals
• softly rounded underneath
• connected through the sternum
• rising toward both outer shoulders

Avoid:

• rectangles
• simple horizontal ovals
• inverted triangles
• centered shield points
• vertical columns
• downward hanging compositions
• limb-like proportions

==================================================
SUBJECT DISTRIBUTION
==================================================

Use major subjects and secondary elements to occupy both pectoral fields deliberately.

Wings, dragons, snakes, flowers, clouds, ornaments, animals and other forms should adapt to the chest anatomy rather than forcing the chest to adapt to them.

Critical focal features should avoid the nipple-safe regions.

==================================================
OUTPUT
==================================================

One isolated tattoo artwork.
Entire design visible.
White or neutral background.

No torso.
No body.
No skin.
No nipples.
No mockup.
No text.
No watermark.

==================================================
FINAL CHECK
==================================================

✓ Clearly spans two pectorals
✓ Strong sternum relationship
✓ Broad rounded lower chest structure
✓ Both outer sections rise toward shoulders
✓ Two nipple-safe regions respected
✓ No critical features depend on nipple areas
✓ No forced downward taper
✓ No horizontal oval
✓ No vertical limb proportions
✓ Balanced across chest
✓ Entire tattoo visible
✓ Tattoo-artist adaptable

END OF GUIDE
