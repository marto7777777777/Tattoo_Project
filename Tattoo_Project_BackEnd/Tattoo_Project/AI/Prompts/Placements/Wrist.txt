WRIST TATTOO DESIGN GUIDE
InkRoute AI Knowledge Base

==================================================
PURPOSE
==================================================

You are currently designing a tattoo specifically intended for the human wrist.

Do NOT generate a wrist.

Do NOT generate a hand.

Do NOT generate skin.

Your task is to create an isolated tattoo artwork whose proportions, simplicity and readability naturally fit the limited space of the wrist.

The final design should immediately feel like it belongs on a wrist without requiring a body mockup.

==================================================
PLACEMENT PRIORITY
==================================================

The wrist is one of the smallest tattoo placements.

Readability is more important than detail.

Simplicity is more important than complexity.

Clean composition is more important than decoration.

==================================================
ANATOMICAL ANALYSIS
==================================================

The wrist is narrow.

The available tattoo area is short.

The cylindrical shape becomes tighter than the forearm.

There is little room for excessive detail.

The tattoo should remain recognizable even when viewed from several meters away.

Never design the tattoo as if it were intended for the forearm.

==================================================
OVERALL SHAPE
==================================================

Preferred silhouettes:

• Slim ribbon

• Vertical ornament

• Small oval

• Compact elongated composition

• Compact botanical composition

Avoid:

• Large rectangles

• Squares

• Wide horizontal layouts

• Dense circular badges

• Large symmetrical back-piece style compositions

==================================================
ASPECT RATIO
==================================================

Preferred ratio

2.8 : 1

Acceptable range

2.5 : 1

to

3.2 : 1

The composition should remain clearly vertical and compact.

==================================================
VISUAL FLOW
==================================================

Movement should remain simple.

The eye should travel naturally from top to bottom.

Avoid unnecessary complexity.

Avoid several competing focal points.

Create one clean visual movement.

==================================================
PRIMARY FOCAL POINT
==================================================

Use one dominant subject only.

The focal point should remain centered.

Avoid oversized decorative frames.

Avoid multiple unrelated subjects.

==================================================
SECONDARY ELEMENTS
==================================================

Supporting elements should remain minimal.

Suitable examples:

• Small leaves

• Fine ornamental lines

• Tiny flowers

• Smoke accents

• Thin geometric elements

Never allow secondary elements to become visually dominant.

==================================================
NEGATIVE SPACE
==================================================

Negative space is essential.

Allow empty areas to improve readability.

Avoid filling every gap.

The silhouette should remain clean.

==================================================
DETAIL DENSITY
==================================================

Low to medium detail.

Avoid tiny textures.

Avoid excessive realism.

Avoid heavy black masses.

Every detail must remain tattooable.

==================================================
SUBJECT SCALING
==================================================

Small flowers should remain proportional.

Minimal snakes should wrap vertically.

Symbols should remain centered.

Lettering should stay readable.

Animals should be simplified if necessary.

Never overcrowd the available space.

==================================================
EDGE TREATMENT
==================================================

Top and bottom may soften or narrow naturally when appropriate to the subject, without forcing pointed spear-like endings.

Avoid abrupt endings.

Maintain elegant transitions.

==================================================
PROFESSIONAL TATTOO PRINCIPLES
==================================================

Design as if the tattoo will be applied tomorrow.

Prioritize longevity.

Prioritize readability.

Prioritize clean execution.

Avoid decorative excess.

Every line should serve a purpose.

==================================================
COMMON AI MISTAKES
==================================================

Never create:

• Overly detailed tattoos

• Tiny unreadable textures

• Multiple focal points

• Wide layouts

• Human hands

• Wrist mockups

• Skin

• Poster compositions

• Cropped artwork

• Decorative overload


==================================================
SILHOUETTE INTERPRETATION
==================================================

Anatomy defines the available proportions, flow and limits of the placement; it does not automatically define one fixed geometric silhouette.

Let the requested subject and tattoo style determine the exact outer contour within those anatomical limits.

Anatomical narrowing should influence the composition subtly and must not automatically create a spear, blade, needle, sharp triangle or pointed tail.

==================================================
ELONGATED PLACEMENT PROPORTION CONTROL
==================================================

This is an elongated anatomical placement. Use its available LENGTH deliberately.

Do not achieve the required vertical proportion by stretching or deforming the primary subject.

Keep the primary subject anatomically natural and use connected secondary elements, supporting forms, controlled negative space, or subject-appropriate extensions to carry the composition through the available length.

Reduced detail toward a narrower anatomical end does NOT mean the composition must continuously lose width.

Do not repeatedly shrink secondary elements until they converge into one centered point.

Do not create a spear, blade, needle, elongated diamond, sharp triangle, shield point, or artificial pointed tail merely because the placement becomes narrower.

The lower or terminal section should retain meaningful visual structure and sufficient width for the placement. It may finish through staggered elements, open negative space, asymmetry, multiple separated endpoints, or a soft organic ending.

A naturally pointed ending is acceptable only when it genuinely belongs to the requested subject itself, such as a snake tail, weapon tip, feather tip, or another inherently pointed form.

Placement anatomy controls overall proportions, usable width, orientation and flow. The requested subject and tattoo style determine the exact outer silhouette.
