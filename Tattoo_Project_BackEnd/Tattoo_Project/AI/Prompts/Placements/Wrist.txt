WRIST TATTOO DESIGN GUIDE
InkRoute AI Knowledge Base

==================================================
PURPOSE
==================================================

You are designing an isolated tattoo specifically intended for the human WRIST.

Do NOT generate a wrist.
Do NOT generate a hand.
Do NOT generate skin.

The wrist is a small, compact tattoo placement.

The design must feel intentionally composed for this limited area rather than appearing like a shortened forearm tattoo.

Prioritize compactness, readability, clean silhouette and immediate visual recognition.

==================================================
PLACEMENT PRIORITY
==================================================

The wrist is NOT an elongated placement.

Do not automatically create vertical compositions.

Do not stretch the design to create artificial length.

Do not use secondary elements merely to extend the composition upward or downward.

The requested subject and tattoo style should determine the exact silhouette within the compact anatomical limits of the wrist.

==================================================
ANATOMICAL ANALYSIS
==================================================

The usable wrist area is relatively short and compact.

Compared with the forearm, there is significantly less available length for composition.

The design should therefore concentrate its visual information into a compact area.

The composition may be:

• near-circular
• oval
• slightly vertically elongated
• slightly horizontally oriented
• compact organic
• compact asymmetrical

depending on the natural structure of the requested subject.

Do not treat the wrist like a miniature forearm.

==================================================
OVERALL SHAPE
==================================================

Preferred silhouettes:

• Compact oval
• Near-circular organic composition
• Short vertical oval
• Compact asymmetrical composition
• Small subject-driven silhouette
• Compact ornamental composition

The exact outer contour should follow the requested subject.

Avoid:

• Long narrow columns
• Tall spear-like compositions
• Forearm-style vertical designs
• Long botanical trails
• Artificial top-to-bottom extensions
• Large rectangular compositions
• Large shield compositions

==================================================
ASPECT RATIO
==================================================

There is no mandatory fixed aspect ratio.

For most wrist designs, keep the overall composition relatively compact.

Typical compositions should remain approximately near-square to moderately elongated.

A useful general range is approximately:

Height : Width

0.8 : 1
to
1.6 : 1

This is guidance, not a forced geometric template.

The subject may exceed this range when its natural anatomy genuinely requires it.

Never distort or stretch the primary subject simply to satisfy a ratio.

==================================================
ORIENTATION
==================================================

Orientation is subject-dependent.

Do NOT automatically favor vertical flow.

A scorpion may use a compact oval composition.

A flower may use a near-circular or softly asymmetrical composition.

A small snake may coil into a compact shape.

A symbol may remain centered and nearly square.

A short dagger may use a moderately vertical composition because the object itself is naturally elongated.

Let the subject determine orientation.

==================================================
PRIMARY FOCAL POINT
==================================================

Use one clearly dominant subject.

The subject should occupy most of the usable composition.

Keep it immediately recognizable.

Do not reduce the main subject simply to make room for decorative elements.

==================================================
SECONDARY ELEMENTS
==================================================

Secondary elements are optional.

Use them only when they improve the requested concept or composition.

Suitable supporting elements may include:

• Small leaves
• Small flowers
• Minimal ornaments
• Small geometric accents
• Limited decorative linework

Do NOT add secondary elements simply to increase the height or width of the tattoo.

Do NOT create long ornamental extensions above or below the main subject unless explicitly requested or naturally required by the concept.

==================================================
NEGATIVE SPACE
==================================================

Negative space is essential at wrist scale.

Preserve clear separation between important forms.

Do not fill every available gap.

Avoid tiny enclosed spaces that will become unreadable over time.

The overall silhouette should remain recognizable from a distance.

==================================================
DETAIL DENSITY
==================================================

Use low to medium overall detail appropriate for the physical size of the wrist.

Concentrate important detail around the focal point.

Simplify secondary structures where necessary.

Avoid excessive microtexture.

Avoid detail that depends on extremely small separation.

Tattoo style should still remain recognizable.

Do not automatically remove strong black areas when the selected tattoo style requires them.

==================================================
SUBJECT SCALING
==================================================

Keep subjects naturally proportioned.

Animals should remain anatomically coherent.

Flowers should remain readable.

Symbols should remain clean.

Lettering must remain legible.

Snakes and scorpions should use their natural curves or coils to remain compact rather than being artificially stretched.

Naturally elongated objects such as daggers, feathers or short ornamental elements may use moderate vertical orientation, but should still feel appropriate for wrist scale.

==================================================
EDGE TREATMENT
==================================================

Edges should follow the natural subject silhouette.

There is no requirement for the top or bottom to narrow.

Do not create artificial pointed endings.

Do not add ornamental tips merely to finish the composition.

Compact rounded, irregular, asymmetrical or naturally open endings are acceptable.

==================================================
PROFESSIONAL TATTOO PRINCIPLES
==================================================

Design as if the tattoo will be applied tomorrow.

Prioritize:

• readability
• longevity
• clean execution
• recognizable silhouette
• appropriate scale
• controlled detail

Every element must justify the limited space it occupies.

==================================================
COMMON AI MISTAKES
==================================================

Never create:

• Forearm-length compositions
• Extremely elongated vertical designs
• Artificial spear shapes
• Long decorative extensions
• Excessive botanical trails
• Tiny unreadable textures
• Multiple competing focal points
• Decorative overload
• Human hands
• Visible wrists
• Skin
• Body mockups
• Poster compositions
• Cropped artwork

==================================================
SILHOUETTE INTERPRETATION
==================================================

The wrist defines a compact available area, not one mandatory geometric silhouette.

The requested subject and tattoo style determine the final outer contour.

Do not force anatomical tapering.

Do not force vertical flow.

Do not force symmetry.

Do not force circularity.

Instead, create the most natural compact silhouette for the requested subject while respecting wrist scale and readability.

==================================================

Generate one isolated tattoo artwork.

Entire tattoo visible.

White or neutral background.

No wrist.

No hand.

No body.

No skin.

No clothing.

No text unless explicitly requested as part of the tattoo.

No watermark.

No logo.
