ONE SIDE UPPER CHEST TATTOO DESIGN GUIDE
InkRoute AI Knowledge Base

==================================================
PURPOSE
==================================================

Design an isolated tattoo specifically intended for ONE SIDE OF THE UPPER CHEST.

Do NOT generate a human body, chest, skin or mockup.

Generate only the tattoo artwork.

The design must naturally follow the anatomical footprint of a single pectoral rather than looking like a generic horizontal tattoo.

==================================================
PLACEMENT PRIORITY
==================================================

The design should occupy one pectoral with a broad, rounded and asymmetrical shape.

It should feel full across the main chest mass.

The lower portion should remain broad and softly rounded.

The outer-upper portion may rise naturally toward the shoulder.

The inner portion near the sternum should remain compact but smoothly rounded.

Never turn the composition into a vertical taper, shield, triangle or pointed chest piece.

==================================================
ANATOMICAL SHAPE
==================================================

A single pectoral is not rectangular and does not continuously narrow downward.

The main chest area has substantial horizontal width.

The lower pectoral contour is broad and rounded.

Toward the sternum, the composition should transition inward through a smooth curve.

Toward the outer-upper chest, the composition may gradually rise toward the shoulder.

This creates an asymmetrical anatomical footprint rather than a symmetrical geometric shape.

==================================================
INNER CHEST / STERNUM SIDE
==================================================

The inner edge must remain softly rounded and anatomically full.

Do NOT create:

• Deep inward indentations

• Concave notches

• Pinched waist shapes

• Hourglass silhouettes

• Bean-shaped contours

• Sudden narrowing near the sternum

Transition gradually from the broad rounded lower chest toward the upper-inner chest.

The composition may become slightly narrower toward the clavicle, but this change must remain smooth and gradual.

==================================================
OUTER CHEST / SHOULDER SIDE
==================================================

The outer-upper portion may rise toward the shoulder.

This rise must happen through a broad, smooth anatomical curve.

Do NOT create:

• Sharp peaks

• Triangular extensions

• Flame-like tips

• Narrow shoulder points

• Sudden upward spikes

Maintain meaningful visual mass while approaching the shoulder.

The shoulder transition should feel like a continuation of the chest rather than a separate pointed extension.

==================================================
LOWER CHEST CONTOUR
==================================================

The lower edge should remain broad, soft and rounded.

Do not continuously taper the composition downward.

Do not create a centered bottom point.

Supporting elements may follow the rounded lower pectoral curve laterally.

The lower silhouette should feel substantial enough to cover the main chest mass.

==================================================
NIPPLE-SAFE COMPOSITION
==================================================

The nipple area must never contain an essential part of the design.

Do NOT place critical features directly through this region, including:

• Faces

• Eyes

• Hands

• Animal heads

• Important symbols

• Essential lettering

• Critical object intersections

Instead, allow this region to contain expendable or interruptible elements such as:

• Soft shading

• Negative space

• Background transitions

• Simple petals

• Leaves

• Clouds

• Waves

• Smoke-like supporting forms when appropriate to the style

The tattoo must remain visually complete if this small area is left untattooed or modified by the tattoo artist.

Do not draw a literal nipple hole or obvious empty circle.

The safe area should be naturally integrated into the composition.

==================================================
OVERALL SHAPE
==================================================

Preferred:

• Broad asymmetrical pectoral composition

• Rounded lower contour

• Smooth sternum-side transition

• Gentle rise toward the shoulder

• Curved ornamental flow

• Lateral subject movement

Avoid:

• Tall narrow compositions

• Shields

• Diamonds

• Triangles

• Vertical columns

• Deep concave sides

• Hourglass silhouettes

• Perfect circles

• Square compositions

==================================================
PROPORTION
==================================================

The design should be clearly wider than tall.

Treat proportions as anatomical guidance rather than a rigid geometric frame.

Prioritize the natural pectoral footprint over forcing an exact aspect ratio.

The composition should use substantial horizontal width without becoming a full-chest design.

==================================================
VISUAL FLOW
==================================================

Favor lateral and gently diagonal movement.

The eye may travel from the inner chest outward and slightly upward toward the shoulder.

Supporting elements should help create this flow.

Avoid strong top-to-bottom sleeve-like movement.

Never stack all major subjects vertically.

==================================================
SUBJECT PLACEMENT
==================================================

Keep the dominant focal point within the main pectoral mass.

Large subjects should use the broad central chest area.

Secondary elements may extend:

• Laterally along the lower chest

• Toward the sternum

• Outward and slightly upward toward the shoulder

Do not force naturally wide subjects into narrow vertical arrangements.

==================================================
DETAIL DISTRIBUTION
==================================================

Highest detail should remain around the primary focal point.

Use medium detail through major supporting elements.

Simplify elements near anatomical boundaries.

Keep the nipple-safe region visually non-critical.

Do not use equal detail everywhere.

==================================================
SILHOUETTE INTERPRETATION
==================================================

Chest anatomy defines the available footprint, but the requested subject and tattoo style determine the exact outer contour.

Do not force every design into the same chest-shaped template.

However, the final silhouette must still respect:

• Broad pectoral width

• Rounded lower anatomy

• Smooth inner transition

• Gradual shoulder rise

• Nipple-safe structure

The composition should look naturally adaptable to one pectoral without requiring a body mockup.

==================================================
COMMON AI MISTAKES
==================================================

Never create:

• Full-chest compositions

• Deep concave inner edges

• Pinched waist silhouettes

• Bean-shaped outlines

• Sharp shoulder peaks

• Pointed lower endings

• Vertical thigh-like taper

• Sleeve-style proportions

• Critical details through the nipple area

• Obvious circular nipple holes

• Torso or skin mockups

• Cropped artwork

==================================================
OUTPUT REQUIREMENTS
==================================================

Generate one isolated tattoo artwork.

Entire tattoo fully visible.

Plain white or very light neutral background.

Clear empty margins.

No body.

No chest.

No skin.

No nipple.

No clothing.

No mockup.

No text unless specifically requested.

No watermark.
