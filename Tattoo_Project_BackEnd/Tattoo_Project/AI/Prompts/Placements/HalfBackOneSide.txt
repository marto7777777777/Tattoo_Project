HALF BACK TATTOO DESIGN GUIDE
InkRoute AI Knowledge Base

==================================================
PURPOSE
==================================================

Design one isolated tattoo specifically intended for HALF BACK placement.

The tattoo must occupy only the LEFT or RIGHT half of the back.

Do NOT generate a human body, back, torso, skin, or mockup.

Generate only the complete isolated tattoo artwork.

==================================================
PLACEMENT PHILOSOPHY
==================================================

Treat HALF BACK as one anatomical half of the actual human back.

It is a LARGE, BROAD, ONE-SIDED BACK CANVAS.

It is NOT a narrow elongated placement.

Do not design it like a forearm, calf, ribs, or vertical poster.

The composition should use substantial width while remaining confined to one side of the back.

==================================================
ANATOMICAL ANALYSIS
==================================================

The half-back canvas is controlled by two fundamentally different boundaries:

1. INNER / SPINE-SIDE BOUNDARY

This boundary runs beside the spinal column.

It should remain approximately straight and vertically stable from the upper back toward the lower back.

It must NOT progressively move inward.

It must NOT curve toward the center near the bottom.

It must NOT participate in a symmetrical taper.

2. OUTER / LATISSIMUS-SIDE BOUNDARY

This boundary follows the natural outer anatomy of the back.

The upper region may expand outward through the trapezius and shoulder-blade area.

The middle back remains broad through the latissimus dorsi region.

Moving downward toward the waist, the OUTER boundary may gradually move inward following the latissimus and waist anatomy.

Therefore:

ANATOMICAL NARROWING HAPPENS PRIMARILY FROM THE OUTER SIDE.

The spine-side boundary remains comparatively stable.

==================================================
ANATOMICAL FOOTPRINT
==================================================

The upper section should have substantial width across the shoulder-blade region.

The middle section should remain broad and visually important.

The lower section may become moderately narrower as the outer contour approaches the waist.

Do NOT make the entire composition progressively narrower from both sides.

The overall footprint should resemble one real anatomical half of a back.

Do not convert this anatomy into a decorative geometric silhouette.

==================================================
OVERALL SHAPE
==================================================

Preferred:

• Broad one-sided back panel
• Anatomical half-back footprint
• Stable vertical spine-side structure
• Broad upper and middle region
• Outer contour following the latissimus
• Organic asymmetrical composition
• Large connected tattoo structure

Avoid:

• Narrow vertical strips
• V-shaped silhouettes
• Diamonds
• Shields
• Spears
• Teardrops
• Long triangles
• Vertical poster shapes
• Symmetrical tapering
• Artificially rounded closed bottoms
• Centered pointed endings

==================================================
PROPORTION
==================================================

The composition should be taller than wide, but NOT dramatically elongated.

Preferred height-to-width relationship:

Approximately 1.4 : 1 to 1.7 : 1

This is a guideline, not a rigid frame.

Subject anatomy and tattoo style may alter the exact silhouette.

Always prioritize the broad anatomical half-back footprint over forcing an exact ratio.

==================================================
VISUAL FLOW
==================================================

Use the full available half-back canvas.

Internal movement may travel:

• Vertically
• Diagonally
• In curves
• Through overlapping subjects
• Through connected background elements

However, internal artistic movement must NOT redefine the entire tattoo into a narrow or tapered silhouette.

Keep the composition visually anchored beside the spine while allowing subjects to expand outward across the shoulder blade and latissimus region.

==================================================
PRIMARY SUBJECT
==================================================

Use one clear dominant focal point or one strongly connected primary scene.

Large subjects should retain believable natural proportions.

Do NOT vertically stretch animals, faces, skulls, masks, characters, or other subjects simply to fill the placement.

Allow large focal subjects to use the width of the back.

==================================================
SECONDARY ELEMENTS
==================================================

Use connected secondary elements to occupy and organize the remaining canvas.

Depending on the requested style, these may include:

• Flowers
• Clouds
• Waves
• Smoke
• Architecture
• Stone
• Foliage
• Ornamental structures
• Graphic elements
• Geometry
• Additional connected subjects

Secondary elements should support the anatomical footprint rather than force everything into one centered vertical column.

==================================================
EDGE BEHAVIOR
==================================================

Do NOT treat the tattoo as one closed decorative shape.

The outer perimeter does not need to form one continuous smooth border.

Individual subjects and supporting elements may end at different heights and positions.

Use staggered endings and controlled negative space where appropriate.

The silhouette should feel naturally created by the tattoo elements themselves.

Do NOT artificially trim the artwork into an oval, shield, diamond, triangle, or decorative panel outline.

==================================================
SPINE-SIDE CONTROL
==================================================

The spine-side edge is the main structural anchor.

Maintain visual presence beside the spine from upper to lower sections.

Do NOT gradually pull every element away from the spine as the composition moves downward.

Do NOT create an inward curve near the lower spine-side area.

Some elements may naturally move away from the spine, but the overall composition must preserve a stable inner boundary.

==================================================
LATISSIMUS-SIDE CONTROL
==================================================

The outer side should reflect the natural contour of the latissimus dorsi.

It may project farther outward through the upper and middle back.

As it approaches the waist, it may gradually become narrower.

This change should be smooth and anatomical.

Do NOT create an abrupt inward notch.

Do NOT create a dramatic concave curve.

Do NOT suddenly collapse the outer width.

==================================================
LOWER SECTION
==================================================

The lower half-back must retain meaningful visual structure.

Do NOT narrow both sides toward one endpoint.

Do NOT make all secondary elements converge.

Do NOT create a V-shaped or pointed bottom.

Do NOT create a rounded bowl-like bottom closure.

The spine-side structure may continue lower while outer elements terminate slightly higher as the anatomy narrows toward the waist.

Different elements may finish at different heights.

The lower boundary may therefore be:

• Staggered
• Softly irregular
• Partially open
• Interrupted by negative space
• Formed by multiple natural endpoints

It does NOT need to become one continuous decorative line.

==================================================
DETAIL DISTRIBUTION
==================================================

Do not use maximum detail everywhere.

Place the highest detail and contrast around the primary focal point.

Use medium detail through important supporting subjects.

Simplify peripheral and transitional elements where appropriate.

Maintain readable separation between overlapping forms.

Use negative space deliberately.

==================================================
STYLE ADAPTATION
==================================================

Placement anatomy controls:

• Available width
• Overall footprint
• Spine-side boundary
• Outer latissimus contour
• General scale
• Usable vertical range

The requested tattoo STYLE controls:

• Linework
• Shading
• Color
• Texture
• Detail density
• Background language
• Edge character

The requested SUBJECT controls the internal silhouette.

Do not force every style or subject into the same geometric outer shape.

==================================================
COMMON AI MISTAKES
==================================================

Never:

• Create a full-back composition
• Create a narrow elongated tattoo
• Treat half back like ribs or forearm
• Taper both sides downward
• Pull the spine-side boundary inward
• Create a strange inward curve near the lower spine
• Create an abrupt notch along the latissimus side
• Create a centered V-shaped bottom
• Create a spear silhouette
• Create a diamond silhouette
• Create a teardrop silhouette
• Create a decorative closed perimeter
• Force every element toward one bottom endpoint
• Stretch the primary subject unnaturally
• Leave excessive unused width
• Generate a body mockup
• Crop the artwork

==================================================
SILHOUETTE INTERPRETATION
==================================================

The human anatomy defines the AVAILABLE CANVAS.

It does NOT define one mandatory decorative silhouette.

Think:

SPINE = stable inner vertical anchor.

SHOULDER BLADE + LATISSIMUS = broad outer canvas.

WAIST = gradual narrowing primarily from the outer side.

The tattoo subjects should be composed INSIDE this anatomical footprint.

Do not transform the entire composition into a generic geometric shape merely to make the artwork look visually complete.
