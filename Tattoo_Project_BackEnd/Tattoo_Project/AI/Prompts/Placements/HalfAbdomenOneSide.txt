HALF ABDOMEN TATTOO DESIGN GUIDE
InkRoute AI Knowledge Base

==================================================
PURPOSE
==================================================

Design one isolated tattoo specifically intended for ONE HALF OF THE ABDOMEN.

Never generate a human body, abdomen, torso, skin, or body mockup.

The design should use the substantial available surface of one side of the abdomen rather than behaving like a narrow vertical limb tattoo.

==================================================
PLACEMENT PRIORITY
==================================================

Prioritize:

• Broad vertical coverage
• Substantial usable width
• One-sided composition
• Balanced top-to-bottom distribution
• Natural abdominal flow

The design should feel made to fill one half of the abdomen.

Do NOT interpret this placement as a long narrow strip.

==================================================
ANATOMICAL FOOTPRINT
==================================================

One half of the abdomen provides a relatively broad and tall surface.

The usable area extends vertically from the upper abdominal region toward the lower abdomen while retaining substantial width throughout most of the placement.

The inner boundary follows the abdominal midline.

The outer boundary may respond gently to the side of the torso.

Do NOT assume that the lower abdomen must become dramatically narrower.

Do NOT continuously reduce width from top to bottom.

==================================================
OVERALL SHAPE
==================================================

The default footprint should resemble a BROAD VERTICAL RECTANGLE with softly organic anatomical edges.

The upper, middle, and lower sections should retain relatively similar visual width.

Small variations in width are allowed when created naturally by the requested subject.

Preferred:

• Broad vertical rectangle
• Soft rectangular composition
• Wide organic vertical panel
• Structured one-sided composition
• Connected composition using most of the available area

Avoid:

• Long narrow strips
• Spears
• Tall diamonds
• Strong triangles
• Long shields
• Narrow botanical ribbons
• Designs that repeatedly taper downward
• Large empty unused side areas

==================================================
PROPORTION CONTROL
==================================================

The design should be only moderately taller than it is wide.

Target approximately:

HEIGHT : WIDTH = 1.4 : 1 to 1.6 : 1

This is NOT a 2:1 or 3:1 elongated placement.

Use both the HEIGHT and WIDTH of the available abdominal area.

==================================================
COMPOSITION
==================================================

Use one dominant focal point or one clearly connected main composition.

The focal subject may occupy the central or upper-middle portion, but supporting elements should expand naturally around it to use the available width.

Do not stretch the primary subject simply to fill the placement.

Use connected secondary elements when necessary to distribute the design across the abdominal footprint.

The composition may be asymmetric because it occupies only one half of the abdomen.

Internal symmetry is allowed when appropriate to the requested subject or style.

==================================================
EDGE BEHAVIOR
==================================================

Keep the inner abdominal edge relatively stable and vertically organized.

Allow the outer edge to have slightly more organic movement.

The lower edge should remain visually substantial.

Do NOT automatically converge the lower section toward one centered point.

Do NOT create an artificial pointed ending unless the requested subject naturally contains one.

==================================================
STYLE ADAPTATION
==================================================

Placement controls the available footprint, not the artistic silhouette.

Geometric designs may use structured rectangular organization.

Realism may use softer irregular edges.

Japanese, illustrative, floral, tribal, blackwork and other flowing styles may break the rectangular boundary organically.

Regardless of style, the design must continue to use the broad abdominal area.

==================================================
COMMON AI MISTAKES
==================================================

Never:

• Draw a body
• Treat half abdomen like a forearm or calf
• Create an excessively elongated tattoo
• Continuously narrow the design downward
• Force a spear or blade silhouette
• Leave most of the available width unused
• Create a full-abdomen symmetrical composition
• Stretch the main subject unnaturally
• Crop the artwork

==================================================
OUTPUT REQUIREMENTS
==================================================

Generate only one isolated tattoo artwork.

Entire tattoo fully visible.

Plain white or very light neutral background.

Clean empty margins.

No body.

No skin.

No mockup.

No text.

No watermark.

==================================================

==================================================

✓ Broad vertical footprint
✓ Approximately 1.4–1.6 : 1 height-to-width
✓ Substantial width retained through the design
✓ No forced downward taper
✓ No artificial pointed ending
✓ Subject remains naturally proportioned
✓ Placement area used efficiently
✓ Entire tattoo visible

✓ Professional tattoo-ready composition
