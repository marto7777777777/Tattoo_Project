FACE TATTOO DESIGN GUIDE
InkRoute AI Knowledge Base

==================================================
PURPOSE
==================================================

Design one isolated tattoo specifically intended for a SMALL FACE placement.

Never generate a human face.

Never generate skin or a body mockup.

Generate only the tattoo artwork itself.

The design should feel appropriate for a small tattoo placed on an open facial area such as the cheek or side of the face.

==================================================
PLACEMENT PRIORITY
==================================================

Face placement is fundamentally different from large anatomical tattoo placements.

Do NOT attempt to cover the entire face.

Do NOT create a face-piece composition.

Do NOT create a large multi-element tattoo.

Treat this placement as one small, intentional standalone tattoo.

Compactness, readability and simplicity have the highest priority.

==================================================
ANATOMICAL SCALE
==================================================

The usable tattoo area is intentionally small.

The design should be suitable for approximately cheek-sized placement or smaller.

The artwork should occupy a compact footprint and remain clearly readable when reduced to real facial tattoo size.

Do not interpret the available facial area as a canvas that must be filled.

Empty surrounding space is expected.

==================================================
OVERALL SHAPE
==================================================

The outer silhouette should be determined naturally by the requested subject.

Suitable forms include:

• Small compact symbol
• Small vertical motif
• Small horizontal motif
• Tiny organic shape
• Small ornamental element
• Simple fine-line illustration

There is no required anatomical taper or fixed geometric silhouette.

Do not artificially stretch or widen the requested subject.

==================================================
COMPOSITION
==================================================

Prefer ONE dominant subject.

If secondary details are necessary, keep them extremely limited and directly connected to the main subject.

The tattoo should usually be understandable immediately from its silhouette.

Avoid complex visual hierarchy.

Avoid multiple focal points.

Avoid large surrounding filler.

Avoid compositions that resemble miniature sleeves or large tattoo panels.

==================================================
DETAIL CONTROL
==================================================

This is critical.

Because the tattoo will be physically small, detail must remain controlled.

Prefer:

• Clear shapes
• Clean line separation
• Simple internal structure
• Limited shading
• Strong readable silhouette
• Intentional negative space

Avoid:

• Micro-textures
• Extremely dense linework
• Excessive stippling
• Tiny overlapping details
• Complex backgrounds
• Numerous small objects
• Detail that would merge when tattooed at small scale

Do not create complexity merely because the selected tattoo style normally supports high detail.

Placement scale has priority over unnecessary detail.

==================================================
LINEWORK
==================================================

Line weight should remain appropriate for the requested style while accounting for the small physical scale.

Fine-line designs may use thin precise lines, but they must remain clearly separated.

Blackwork may use stronger shapes and small solid-black areas.

Traditional or similar styles may use bolder outlines, but the composition must remain compact.

Avoid excessive line density.

==================================================
SHADING
==================================================

Keep shading controlled.

Large smooth gradients and extremely complex tonal rendering are usually unnecessary at this scale.

Use shading only when it improves readability and supports the requested style.

Maintain enough negative space for the tattoo to remain readable over time.

==================================================
SUBJECT ADAPTATION
==================================================

The requested subject should be simplified appropriately for face-tattoo scale.

Examples:

A spider:
Use a clearly readable body and legs with controlled detail.

A rose:
Use a small number of clearly separated petals rather than excessive petal complexity.

A snake:
Use a compact curved or coiled pose rather than a long sleeve-like composition.

A skull:
Use a small simplified skull with strong recognizable structure rather than highly detailed realism.

A symbol:
Keep the geometry clean and immediately readable.

A word or lettering:
Keep it short and legible.

==================================================
STYLE INTERPRETATION
==================================================

The selected tattoo style should still be recognizable, but it must adapt to the small placement.

Do not force the normal complexity of a large tattoo style into a tiny face design.

For realism:
Use simplified black-and-grey realism with one small recognizable subject and controlled texture.

For blackwork:
Use bold clean shapes and intentional negative space.

For fine line:
Use delicate but clearly separated lines.

For traditional styles:
Use a simplified compact version of the style with strong readability.

Placement scale overrides unnecessary stylistic complexity.

==================================================
POOR CHOICES
==================================================

Avoid:

✗ Full portraits

✗ Large animal heads

✗ Full dragons

✗ Samurai scenes

✗ Battle scenes

✗ Large mandalas

✗ Large sacred geometry

✗ Multi-character compositions

✗ Sleeve-like compositions

✗ Large landscapes

✗ Highly complex realism scenes

If the requested concept is naturally too complex, simplify it into one recognizable focal subject rather than creating a miniature large-scale tattoo.

==================================================
COMMON AI MISTAKES
==================================================

Never create:

• a human face
• skin
• eyes, nose, lips or ears as placement references
• a body mockup
• an oversized tattoo
• a full-face composition
• a large vertical panel
• a sleeve-like design
• multiple unrelated subjects
• excessive background decoration
• excessive micro-detail
• poster artwork
• unnecessary filler

Do not enlarge the concept merely to showcase more detail.

==================================================
OUTPUT REQUIREMENTS
==================================================

Generate:

One isolated small tattoo artwork.

Plain white or very light neutral background.

Entire tattoo fully visible.

Clear empty margin surrounding the artwork.

No human face.

No skin.

No body.

No placement mockup.

No text unless specifically requested.

No watermark.

No logo.
