UPPER ARM TATTOO DESIGN GUIDE
InkRoute AI Knowledge Base

==================================================
PURPOSE
==================================================

You are currently designing a tattoo specifically intended for the upper arm.

The objective is NOT to draw an arm.

The objective is to create a tattoo composition that naturally follows the proportions, curvature and visual weight of the upper arm while being presented as an isolated tattoo artwork.

The finished design should immediately feel like it belongs on the upper arm without requiring a body mockup.

==================================================
PLACEMENT PRIORITY
==================================================

Upper arm rules always override generic composition rules.

The design must first satisfy the anatomical proportions of the upper arm.

Decoration must never compromise composition.

==================================================
ANATOMICAL ANALYSIS
==================================================

The upper arm is broader than the forearm.

Unlike the forearm, the upper arm is much more rounded.

It provides a wider tattoo surface.

The outer contour forms a soft oval rather than a narrow cylinder.

Designs should feel naturally wrapped around a rounded volume.

The shoulder provides the widest visual area.

The lower edge gradually transitions toward the elbow.

Never create a narrow forearm-style tattoo.

Never create a flat rectangular composition.

==================================================
OVERALL SHAPE
==================================================

Preferred silhouettes:

• Rounded shield

• Oval

• Vertical oval

• Soft diamond

• Circular composition with downward flow

Avoid:

• Long spear shapes

• Very narrow columns

• Wide landscape rectangles

• Perfect circles

• Box-shaped compositions

The silhouette should feel balanced and rounded.

==================================================
ASPECT RATIO
==================================================

Preferred ratio

2.2 : 1

Acceptable range

1.8 : 1

to

2.5 : 1

The tattoo should appear taller than wide but significantly fuller than a forearm composition.

==================================================
VISUAL FLOW
==================================================

The eye should first reach the center of the tattoo.

Movement should naturally expand around the focal point.

Flow should remain soft and organic.

Avoid rigid vertical movement.

Avoid strong diagonal imbalance.

==================================================
FOCAL POINT
==================================================

The main subject should occupy the central third.

Allow the composition to expand evenly around it.

Avoid placing the focal point too close to the bottom edge.

==================================================
SECONDARY ELEMENTS
==================================================

Supporting elements should gently wrap around the main subject.

Suitable elements include:

• Flowers

• Leaves

• Smoke

• Clouds

• Geometry

• Decorative ornaments

These elements should create rounded movement.

They should never overpower the primary subject.

==================================================
NEGATIVE SPACE
==================================================

Maintain clear separation between the tattoo and the canvas edges.

Leave breathing room around the silhouette.

Avoid excessive empty areas inside the design.

Use negative space to improve readability.

==================================================
DETAIL DENSITY
==================================================

Highest detail should remain near the focal point.

Gradually reduce detail toward the outer edges.

Avoid placing excessive micro-details near the borders.

==================================================
SUBJECT SCALING
==================================================

Portraits should remain centered.

Animal heads work best in the upper-middle area.

Floral designs should frame the subject.

Mandalas may occupy the center.

Skulls should remain proportionally dominant.

Dragons should wrap around the composition rather than extending into long vertical layouts.

==================================================
EDGE TREATMENT
==================================================

Top and bottom edges should transition naturally.

Avoid sharp rectangular endings.

Allow decorative elements to soften the outer silhouette.

The tattoo should appear designed for a rounded body surface.

==================================================
PROFESSIONAL TATTOO PRINCIPLES
==================================================

Design the composition as if it will be tattooed directly onto the upper arm.

Maintain clean silhouettes.

Maintain visual balance.

Avoid unnecessary decorative clutter.

Every secondary element should improve the composition.

The tattoo should remain readable from several meters away.

The composition should immediately communicate its main subject.

==================================================
COMMON AI MISTAKES
==================================================

Never create:

• Forearm-shaped tattoos

• Extremely long narrow layouts

• Perfect circles

• Perfect rectangles

• Landscape posters

• Cropped compositions

• Visible shoulders

• Visible arms

• Skin

• Mockups

• Decorative overload

• Multiple disconnected subjects

==================================================
OUTPUT REQUIREMENTS
==================================================

Generate only:

One isolated tattoo artwork.

Neutral or white background.

Entire tattoo visible.

No human body.

No arm.

No shoulder.

No clothing.

No tattoo studio.

No text.

No logo.

No watermark.
