NECK TATTOO DESIGN GUIDE
InkRoute AI Knowledge Base

==================================================
PURPOSE
==================================================

Design one isolated tattoo specifically intended for the FULL FRONT OF THE NECK.

Never generate a neck, body, skin or tattoo mockup.

The isolated artwork itself must have an outer silhouette that matches the usable anatomical area of the front neck.

The placement shape is a major requirement.

==================================================
PLACEMENT PRIORITY
==================================================

The tattoo should occupy the full front-neck area:

• from directly beneath the jaw and chin
• across the complete visible width of the front neck
• down to the base of the throat
• with the lower edges naturally following the collarbone directions

Do not design this as a small neck tattoo.

Do not design this as a narrow vertical ornament.

Do not design this as a chest piece.

The finished isolated artwork should immediately look shaped for a full front-neck tattoo.

==================================================
CORE SILHOUETTE
==================================================

The overall footprint should resemble a compact anatomical panel.

Through most of its height, maintain substantial and relatively consistent width.

The basic structure is approximately rectangular through the central area, but BOTH the top and bottom boundaries must be anatomically curved.

The silhouette must be determined by:

TOP:
a pronounced inward curve beneath the chin

SIDES:
strong, mostly vertical side boundaries

BOTTOM:
a broad curved transition following the base of the throat and collarbone directions

Do NOT use a shield silhouette.

Do NOT use a diamond silhouette.

Do NOT use a triangle.

Do NOT use a narrow vertical oval.

Do NOT progressively taper the complete composition toward one point.

==================================================
TOP EDGE — FOLLOW THE JAW
==================================================

This is critical.

The upper boundary of the isolated tattoo must visually follow the underside of the jaw.

The tattoo should rise highest at the LEFT and RIGHT upper corners where the neck meets the jaw.

From both upper corners, the boundary must curve DOWNWARD toward the center underneath the chin.

This creates a broad, smooth, clearly visible CONCAVE upper edge.

LEFT JAW SIDE = HIGH

CENTER UNDER CHIN = LOWER

RIGHT JAW SIDE = HIGH

The result should resemble a wide U-shaped cutout in the TOP of the tattoo.

The central upper indentation must be broad enough to represent the space occupied by the underside of the chin.

Do not make the top edge flat.

Do not make it convex.

Do not create a pointed central notch.

Do not place a decorative peak above the center.

The artwork itself must visibly contain this anatomical upper cutout.

ABSOLUTE UPPER BOUNDARY RULE

The concave jaw-following contour defines the actual outer boundary of the entire tattoo.

No major subject, wing, horn, feather, ornament, crown, weapon, smoke, floral element, or supporting structure may project above this anatomical boundary.

Supporting elements must bend, rotate, spread sideways, or terminate inside the available jaw-shaped footprint.

Do not interpret "higher near the left and right jaw corners" as permission to create tall upward projections.

The two upper outer corners may rise moderately, but the complete top silhouette must still read as one broad anatomical concave curve designed to sit directly beneath the jaw.

When the requested subject naturally contains tall elements, adapt their orientation to the neck placement instead of allowing them to break the placement silhouette.

==================================================
SIDE EDGES
==================================================

After the upper jaw corners, both outer sides should descend predominantly downward.

The side boundaries should remain relatively vertical through most of the composition.

They may have subtle organic irregularities caused by the requested subject and style.

However, the overall width must remain substantial.

Do not continuously pull both sides inward toward the bottom.

Do not create an hourglass.

Do not create a V-shaped silhouette.

Do not create a shield.

Do not create a long taper.

==================================================
BOTTOM EDGE — THROAT AND COLLARBONE FLOW
==================================================

The lower boundary must follow the transition between the base of the throat and the collarbone region.

Unlike the upper boundary, the lower center may extend LOWER than the outer edges.

The left and right lower edges should naturally spread slightly outward toward the collarbone directions.

The bottom contour should therefore form a broad, shallow downward curve.

LEFT COLLARBONE SIDE = slightly higher

CENTER BASE OF THROAT = lower

RIGHT COLLARBONE SIDE = slightly higher

The center may descend moderately toward the suprasternal notch, but it must remain broad and rounded.

Do NOT turn this into a long central point.

Do NOT create a V-shaped ending.

Do NOT create a spear tip.

Do NOT create a diamond bottom.

Do NOT extend the artwork far onto the chest.

The bottom should feel like it rests naturally across the base of the neck.

==================================================
TARGET OUTER SHAPE
==================================================

Imagine the complete tattoo as a front-neck panel with this approximate behavior:

        HIGH               HIGH
          \               /
           \_____   _____/
                 \_/
             UNDER CHIN

        |               |
        |               |
        |               |
        |               |
        |               |
         \             /
          \___________/
        COLLARBONE FLOW

This diagram describes ONLY the placement footprint.

Do not literally create a geometric border.

The requested tattoo subject and style must naturally fill this anatomical footprint.

==================================================
WIDTH DISTRIBUTION
==================================================

Maintain substantial width through approximately 70–80% of the tattoo height.

The design should NOT begin wide and continuously narrow.

The design should NOT begin narrow and continuously widen.

The main central neck area is relatively consistent in width.

Only the anatomical top and bottom boundaries should strongly modify the silhouette.

==================================================
ASPECT RATIO
==================================================

The full front-neck tattoo should be moderately vertical but not extremely elongated.

Target approximate overall proportion:

Height : Width = 1.2 : 1 to 1.5 : 1

Do not create a 2:1 or 3:1 narrow vertical composition.

Do not create a wide horizontal chest-piece composition.

==================================================
SUBJECT ADAPTATION
==================================================

The requested subject must adapt to the neck footprint.

For a large animal head:

• face occupies the central area
• ears, horns, feathers, shadows or supporting forms may rise toward the jaw corners
• cheeks, fur, feathers or supporting elements may fill the side boundaries
• lower fur, shadows, ornaments or connected elements may follow the collarbone curve

For a skull or mask:

• main face remains central
• surrounding structures complete the upper jaw-side corners
• lower supporting forms complete the base-of-neck curve

For abstract or ornamental designs:

• ornamentation should deliberately follow the same anatomical boundaries

Never distort important anatomy merely to fill the shape.

Use connected secondary structure when necessary.

==================================================
REALISM AND HEAVY STYLES
==================================================

Full front-neck placement can support strong visual mass.

Realism portraits, animal heads, skulls, masks, blackwork and other dark subjects are allowed.

Do not automatically make neck tattoos delicate, minimal or lightweight.

When the requested style supports it, deep black saturation and strong contrast are appropriate.

Maintain enough negative space and tonal separation for tattoo readability.

==================================================
DETAIL DISTRIBUTION
==================================================

The main focal subject should contain the highest detail.

Supporting structures near the outer boundaries should use slightly reduced detail.

Do not fill every available area with micro-detail.

The tattoo should remain readable at real neck scale.

==================================================
COMMON AI MISTAKES
==================================================

Never create:

• a human neck
• a body mockup
• skin
• a narrow vertical ornament
• a shield
• a diamond
• a triangle
• a pointed lower ending
• a flat upper edge
• a convex upper edge
• a decorative peak above the center
• a long chest extension
• an extremely wide chest piece
• a small floating central tattoo
• continuous taper from top to bottom

The isolated tattoo itself must visibly communicate the front-neck anatomy through its outer silhouette.

==================================================
OUTPUT REQUIREMENTS
==================================================

Generate:

One isolated tattoo artwork.

Entire artwork visible.

Plain white or very light neutral background.

Clear empty margin around the complete artwork.

No human body.

No neck.

No skin.

No clothing.

No mockup.

No text unless specifically requested.

No watermark.

No logo.
