FULL BACK TATTOO DESIGN GUIDE
InkRoute AI Knowledge Base

==================================================
PURPOSE
==================================================

You are currently designing a tattoo specifically intended for the FULL BACK.

Your task is NOT to generate a human back.

Your task is to create an isolated tattoo composition that naturally follows the proportions, movement and visual balance of the human back.

The final artwork should immediately feel like it was designed specifically for the back.

==================================================
PLACEMENT PRIORITY
==================================================

Back anatomy has higher priority than generic tattoo composition rules.

The composition must always respect the natural widening of the shoulders and the gradual narrowing toward the waist.

Never optimize the artwork as if it were a poster.

==================================================
ANATOMICAL ANALYSIS
==================================================

The back is the largest tattoo placement on the human body.

The shoulders provide the greatest usable width.

The torso gradually narrows toward the waist.

The center line of the spine acts as the natural visual axis.

Large tattoo compositions should follow this anatomical flow.

Never maintain identical width from top to bottom.

Never create a perfect rectangle.

Never create a square layout.

==================================================
OVERALL SHAPE
==================================================

Preferred silhouettes:

• Shield

• Tapered diamond

• Cathedral

• Vertical crest

• Wide upper triangle with soft taper

Avoid:

• Rectangle

• Perfect square

• Landscape poster

• Equal-width column

• Circular badge

The tattoo should always appear wider at the top than at the bottom.

==================================================
ASPECT RATIO
==================================================

Preferred ratio:

Height : Width

1.3 : 1

Acceptable range:

1.2 : 1

to

1.5 : 1

The tattoo should feel large, dominant and vertically structured.

==================================================
VISUAL FLOW
==================================================

The viewer's eye should begin near the upper center.

The composition should naturally travel downward.

Visual movement should remain continuous.

Avoid abrupt visual interruptions.

Never split the tattoo into unrelated sections.

==================================================
PRIMARY FOCAL POINT
==================================================

Place the dominant focal point within the upper third.

Examples:

• Dragon head

• Tiger face

• Samurai

• Skull

• Phoenix

• Oni mask

Supporting elements should guide the eye downward.

==================================================
SECONDARY ELEMENTS
==================================================

Use secondary elements to reinforce movement.

Examples:

• Smoke

• Clouds

• Waves

• Wind bars

• Flowers

• Snakes

• Ornamental geometry

These elements should connect the complete composition.

Never let them overpower the main subject.

==================================================
NEGATIVE SPACE
==================================================

Maintain readable separation between major forms.

Avoid filling every available space.

Allow breathing room around the silhouette.

Negative space should improve readability rather than create empty gaps.

==================================================
DETAIL DENSITY
==================================================

The upper half should contain the highest level of detail.

Reduce detail gradually toward the lower back.

Large decorative masses should become lighter toward the bottom.

==================================================
WEIGHT DISTRIBUTION
==================================================

Visual weight should remain strongest across the shoulders.

The composition should naturally become lighter toward the waist.

Avoid placing the heaviest element at the bottom.

The tattoo should feel stable and balanced.

==================================================
SUBJECT SCALING
==================================================

Large animals should dominate the upper half.

Portraits should remain centered.

Wings should naturally expand toward the shoulders.

Dragons should travel vertically.

Snakes should create flowing movement.

Floral compositions should frame rather than dominate.

==================================================
EDGE TREATMENT
==================================================

The upper silhouette may expand broadly.

The lower silhouette should taper naturally.

Avoid flat horizontal endings.

Decorative elements should soften the overall outline.

==================================================
PROFESSIONAL TATTOO PRINCIPLES
==================================================

The design should immediately communicate that it belongs on a full back.

Maintain a strong central axis.

Maintain readable silhouettes.

Avoid visual clutter.

Avoid unnecessary filler.

Every element should improve the composition.

Design the artwork as though it will become a lifetime tattoo.

==================================================
COMMON AI MISTAKES
==================================================

Never create:

• Square layouts

• Equal-width compositions

• Poster-like artwork

• Human backs

• Skin

• Mockups

• Cropped wings

• Cropped dragons

• Cropped flowers

• Empty lower sections

• Decorative overload

• Multiple disconnected compositions

==================================================
OUTPUT REQUIREMENTS
==================================================

Generate only:

One isolated tattoo artwork.

Entire composition visible.

Neutral or white background.

No human body.

No back.

No skin.

No clothing.

No tattoo studio.

No text.

No watermark.

No logo.
