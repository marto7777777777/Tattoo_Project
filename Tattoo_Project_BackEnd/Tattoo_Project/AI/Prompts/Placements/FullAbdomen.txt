FULL ABDOMEN TATTOO DESIGN GUIDE
InkRoute AI Knowledge Base

==================================================
PURPOSE
==================================================

Design one isolated tattoo specifically intended for the FULL ABDOMEN.

Do NOT generate an abdomen, torso, body, skin, or body mockup.

Create a broad central tattoo composition capable of occupying the abdominal area from side to side while using its available vertical space.

==================================================
PLACEMENT PRIORITY
==================================================

Prioritize:

• Broad abdominal coverage
• Strong central organization
• Balanced use of width and height
• Stable visual mass
• Natural adaptation to abdominal anatomy

The abdomen is a large surface.

Do NOT treat it as a narrow vertical placement.

==================================================
ANATOMICAL FOOTPRINT
==================================================

The full abdomen provides substantial width across both sides of the central abdominal line.

The middle abdominal region retains significant usable width.

The lower abdomen may change shape slightly, but it should NOT automatically cause the entire tattoo to taper dramatically downward.

The central abdominal line can act as a compositional axis when appropriate.

The design should use both sides of this axis.

==================================================
OVERALL SHAPE
==================================================

The default footprint should resemble a BROAD, MODERATELY TALL RECTANGULAR OR SOFTLY STRUCTURED ABDOMINAL PANEL.

The design may have organic edges and anatomical variation, but its main visual mass should remain broad.

Preferred:

• Broad vertical rectangle
• Soft rectangular panel
• Wide ornamental composition
• Broad structured frame
• Large centered organic composition
• Wide symmetrical or asymmetrical composition

Avoid:

• Narrow shields
• Tall diamonds
• Cathedral-like pointed silhouettes by default
• Long vertical columns
• Spear shapes
• Strong triangular taper
• Designs concentrated only around the centerline

==================================================
PROPORTION CONTROL
==================================================

Use substantial width.

The full abdomen should NOT appear dramatically taller than it is wide.

Target approximately:

HEIGHT : WIDTH = 1.1 : 1 to 1.35 : 1

Exact proportions may vary slightly depending on the requested subject and style.

The important rule is:

USE THE WIDTH OF THE ABDOMEN.

Do not compress a large design into a narrow central strip.

==================================================
COMPOSITION
==================================================

The design may use:

• One large central focal point
• A central subject with connected lateral elements
• Two interacting subjects
• A structured symmetrical composition
• A broad organic composition spanning both sides

The central axis may organize the artwork, but it must not force everything into a narrow vertical stack.

Supporting elements should distribute visual weight across the abdomen.

Keep the primary subject anatomically natural.

Do not stretch subjects vertically merely to fill space.

==================================================
SYMMETRY
==================================================

Symmetry is suitable for many full-abdomen designs but is NOT mandatory.

Geometric, ornamental, tribal and similar styles may use strong symmetry.

Realism, Japanese, illustrative, biomechanical, Trash Polka and other styles may use controlled asymmetry.

The placement requires BALANCE, not compulsory mirror symmetry.

==================================================
NAVEL-SAFE COMPOSITION
==================================================

The central abdominal area may contain the navel when applied to the body.

Do not make one tiny critical feature dependent on the exact central point of the design.

When possible, allow the central region to contain:

• Negative space
• Background shading
• Pattern transitions
• Secondary texture
• Non-critical supporting structure

The design should remain visually coherent if a tattoo artist modifies or interrupts a small central area during application.

Do not draw a navel.

==================================================
EDGE BEHAVIOR
==================================================

Maintain substantial width through the middle and lower portions.

The sides may curve or contract gently according to the subject.

Avoid automatic continuous tapering.

Avoid forcing both lower sides to converge toward a single point.

The bottom may finish broadly, organically, asymmetrically, or through several connected endpoints depending on the design.

==================================================
STYLE ADAPTATION
==================================================

The abdominal footprint defines available space, not one mandatory silhouette.

Geometric designs may remain structured and rectangular.

Japanese designs may create broad flowing edges.

Realism may use soft irregular transitions.

Tribal and ornamental styles may follow stronger controlled contours.

Floral and illustrative designs may break the boundary organically.

Every style should still use the substantial WIDTH of the full abdomen.

==================================================
COMMON AI MISTAKES
==================================================

Never:

• Generate a torso or body
• Treat full abdomen like a forearm
• Produce a narrow vertical column
• Automatically create a shield
• Automatically create a diamond
• Continuously taper toward the bottom
• Force a pointed lower ending
• Place all important content on the centerline
• Stretch subjects vertically
• Ignore the available abdominal width
• Crop the artwork

==================================================
OUTPUT REQUIREMENTS
==================================================

Generate one isolated tattoo artwork.

Entire tattoo fully visible.

Plain white or very light neutral background.

Clean margins.

No abdomen.

No torso.

No skin.

No body.

No mockup.

No text.

No watermark.
