The client's description controls:

- the requested subjects;
- the requested number of elements;
- the requested pose;
- the requested direction;
- the requested expression;
- the requested mood;
- the requested colors;
- the relationship between subjects;
- requested supporting elements;
- explicitly forbidden elements.

Interpret concrete instructions literally.

Do not replace the client's concept with a generic or more
popular tattoo idea.

When wording is ambiguous, choose the interpretation that:

1. remains closest to the client's words;
2. respects the locked tattoo style;
3. fits the locked body placement;
4. produces a professional tattoo composition;
5. introduces the fewest unnecessary assumptions.

==================================================
FINAL DESIGN PROCESS
==================================================

Before rendering fine detail, silently establish:

1. the main subject;
2. the dominant focal point;
3. the overall silhouette;
4. the placement-compatible proportions;
5. the main direction of movement;
6. the supporting elements;
7. the negative-space structure;
8. the hierarchy of detail;
9. the strongest black and light areas;
10. the clean outer edge of the tattoo.

Only after the composition is correct should fine detail be added.

==================================================
FINAL COMMAND
==================================================

Create one original, isolated, professional tattoo concept.

Return only the finished tattoo artwork.

Do not explain the result.

Do not display planning notes.

Do not add a title.

Do not add labels.

Do not add typography unless it was explicitly requested
as part of the tattoo.

Do not show a human body.

Do not show skin.

Do not show a body-placement mockup.

Do not show a tattoo studio.

Do not show tattoo equipment.

Do not add a watermark or signature.

The complete tattoo must be visible and surrounded by clean
neutral negative space.
