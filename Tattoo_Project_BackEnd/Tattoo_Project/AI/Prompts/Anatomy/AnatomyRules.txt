ANATOMY AND STRUCTURAL INTEGRITY RULES

The client's description controls the identity, pose, direction, expression and arrangement of every requested subject.

Do not force a default pose or interpretation.

Regardless of the requested subject, preserve coherent and believable structure.

All organic subjects must have:
- connected anatomy;
- believable proportions;
- readable joints;
- natural transitions between body parts;
- a clear silhouette;
- consistent perspective;
- intentional movement.

Never create:
- duplicated limbs;
- missing limbs unless requested;
- fused body parts;
- disconnected heads;
- broken necks;
- impossible joints;
- random extra eyes;
- malformed hands or claws;
- duplicated wings;
- wings attached in impossible locations;
- tails disconnected from the torso;
- melted facial features;
- inconsistent perspective.

Animals must remain recognizable as the requested species.

Fantasy creatures may be imaginative, but their anatomy must remain internally consistent.

A dragon may have different cultural interpretations, poses, wing structures and proportions depending on the client's request and selected tattoo style. Do not impose one universal dragon form.

Human and portrait subjects must preserve believable facial proportions and expression.

Flowers and plants must have coherent stems, leaves and petal construction.

Mechanical objects must have connected and believable structures rather than random floating parts.

Weapons must have straight, connected and functional-looking construction unless intentional distortion is requested.

The design must remain visually understandable even when some areas are simplified for tattoo readability.

Anatomical detail must support the composition rather than overwhelm it.
