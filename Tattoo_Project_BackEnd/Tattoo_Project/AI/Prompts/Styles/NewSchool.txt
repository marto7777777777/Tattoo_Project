NEW SCHOOL TATTOO STYLE GUIDE
InkRoute AI Knowledge Base

==================================================
PURPOSE
==================================================

Create authentic professional New School tattoo artwork designed for real tattoo execution.

The artwork should feel exaggerated, energetic, colorful and full of personality while remaining clearly tattooable.

Never sacrifice tattoo readability or practical execution for digital illustration effects.

==================================================
STYLE PHILOSOPHY
==================================================

New School uses:

• Exaggerated proportions
• Expressive characters
• Bold silhouettes
• Dynamic movement
• Strong outlines
• Saturated tattoo colors
• Playful visual distortion

Exaggeration should come primarily from shape, pose, expression and proportion — not from excessive rendering or digital effects.

The result must look like tattoo artwork, not concept art, game art, sticker art or a digital painting.

==================================================
LINE WORK
==================================================

Use bold, confident outlines.

Use controlled variation in line weight.

Primary subjects should have the strongest structural outlines.

Interior lines may be thinner but must remain clearly tattooable.

Avoid excessive tiny lines.

Avoid hair-thin decorative detail.

Avoid outlining every small texture.

Use linework to create large readable forms.

==================================================
SHAPE LANGUAGE
==================================================

Favor large, intentional shapes.

Simplify complicated anatomy and textures into readable tattoo forms.

Exaggerate important features such as:

• Eyes
• Mouths
• Teeth
• Expressions
• Hands or claws
• Major curves
• Character silhouettes

Do not fill every surface with small details.

The design must remain recognizable from a distance.

==================================================
COLOR PALETTE
==================================================

Use saturated but physically believable tattoo ink colors.

Suitable colors include:

• Red
• Orange
• Yellow
• Green
• Blue
• Purple
• Pink
• Black

Use a controlled palette appropriate to the requested subject.

Prefer approximately 3–5 dominant colors plus black rather than using every available color.

Colors should appear like real deposited tattoo pigments, not emitted digital light.

Bright colors are encouraged, but they must remain believable on tattooed skin.

==================================================
COLOR RESTRICTIONS
==================================================

Never create:

• Neon RGB glow
• Luminous edges
• Electric light effects
• Fluorescent-looking gradients
• Digital bloom
• Colored rim lighting around every object
• Rainbow shading across every surface
• Excessive blue-purple-pink transitions
• Dozens of tiny color changes
• Artificial glowing eyes unless specifically requested

Do not make black surfaces appear purple or blue simply because the design is colorful.

Black should remain visually black unless deliberate reflected color is compositionally necessary.

==================================================
SHADING
==================================================

Use clean tattoo-oriented shading.

Favor:

• Solid color fields
• Controlled gradients
• Deliberate black shadows
• Simple dimensional transitions
• Clear highlight areas
• Intentional skin or negative-space breaks

Avoid digital airbrush rendering.

Avoid hundreds of tiny highlight strokes.

Avoid extremely smooth multi-color transitions that depend on digital rendering.

Shading must support large readable forms.

==================================================
DETAIL DENSITY
==================================================

Use medium detail overall.

Highest detail belongs to the dominant character's face and important expressive features.

Secondary elements should use less detail.

Outer edges should become simpler.

Do not render individual hairs, scales, flames, wrinkles or textures excessively.

Group repeated textures into larger tattoo-readable patterns.

Every visible detail should have a realistic purpose in the final tattoo.

==================================================
COMPOSITION
==================================================

Use one dominant character or subject.

Create dynamic movement appropriate to the requested placement.

Supporting elements may include:

• Flames
• Clouds
• Skulls
• Flowers
• Graffiti-inspired forms
• Smoke
• Cartoon objects
• Abstract shapes

Supporting elements must remain secondary.

Do not overcrowd the composition simply because New School allows creative freedom.

Placement anatomy determines the overall available proportions and flow.

The style should adapt to the placement rather than forcing every design into the same cartoon silhouette.

==================================================
NEGATIVE SPACE
==================================================

Preserve clear negative-space separation between important forms.

Do not fill every gap with color or decoration.

Use open areas to separate:

• Limbs
• Facial features
• Flames
• Secondary objects
• Overlapping shapes

The design should remain readable after years of aging in skin.

==================================================
TATTOO EXECUTION CONTROL
==================================================

Design as if a professional tattoo artist must reproduce the artwork on skin tomorrow.

Avoid illustration-level micro-detail.

Avoid effects that depend on screen brightness.

Avoid extremely small isolated highlights.

Avoid tiny color gradients.

Avoid unnecessarily complicated textures.

Use sufficiently large shapes, readable outlines and practical color transitions.

The artwork may be visually complex, but its construction must remain tattooable.

==================================================
VISUAL MOOD
==================================================

New School may feel:

• Playful
• Crazy
• Aggressive
• Humorous
• Energetic
• Colorful
• Expressive
• Surreal

The exact mood should follow the user's request.

Do not automatically make every New School tattoo childish or comedic.

==================================================

✓ Monsters
✓ Skulls
✓ Mushrooms
✓ Frogs
✓ Foxes
✓ Dragons
✓ Characters
✓ Food
✓ Graffiti-inspired subjects
✓ Exaggerated objects

==================================================
COMMON AI MISTAKES
==================================================

Never:

• Create digital concept art instead of tattoo artwork
• Use neon glow as the primary source of color
• Cover dark subjects with purple and blue highlights
• Create excessive micro-detail
• Use dozens of tiny gradients
• Add unnecessary rainbow colors
• Make every surface glossy
• Overcrowd secondary elements
• Lose the silhouette in decorative effects
• Create weak unreadable outlines
• Create stiff lifeless characters
• Create body mockups

==================================================
OUTPUT REQUIREMENTS
==================================================

Generate one isolated professional tattoo artwork.

Entire tattoo visible.

Plain white or very light neutral background.

No body.

No skin.

No mockup.

No text unless specifically requested.

No watermark.

No logo.

✓ Placement-appropriate composition

✓ Entire artwork visible

✓ Realistically executable as a tattoo
