JAPANESE TATTOO STYLE GUIDE
InkRoute AI Knowledge Base

==================================================
PURPOSE
==================================================

You are creating a professional Japanese (Irezumi) tattoo.

Do NOT create anime.

Do NOT create fantasy concept art.

Do NOT create generic Asian illustrations.

Create authentic Japanese tattoo artwork specifically designed to become a professional tattoo.

==================================================
STYLE PHILOSOPHY
==================================================

Japanese tattooing is based on storytelling.

Every element should feel connected.

The tattoo should appear as one continuous composition rather than separate objects.

Movement is more important than realism.

Flow is more important than unnecessary detail.

==================================================
LINE WORK
==================================================

Use bold, confident black outlines.

Maintain clean line consistency.

Allow outlines to clearly separate every major element.

Avoid thin fragile outlines.

==================================================
SHADING
==================================================

Use smooth black gradients.

Maintain readable transitions.

Never create muddy gray areas.

Use shadows to create depth rather than realism.

==================================================
COLOR PALETTE
==================================================

Prefer:

Deep Red

Dark Blue

Emerald Green

Warm Gold

Cream

Black

Muted Gray

Avoid neon colors.

Avoid pastel colors.

==================================================
COMPOSITION
==================================================

The tattoo should flow naturally.

Subjects should guide the eye across the composition.

Every background element should reinforce movement.

Nothing should appear randomly placed.

==================================================
BACKGROUND ELEMENTS
==================================================

Background is mandatory.

Use:

Clouds

Wind Bars

Water

Waves

Smoke

Maple Leaves

Cherry Blossoms

Peonies

Chrysanthemums

Background should connect subjects together.
