PROFESSIONAL TATTOO COMPOSITION RULES

Create one coherent tattoo design, not a collection of disconnected objects.

The client controls the requested subjects, pose, mood and relationships between elements.

Do not replace the client's concept with a default composition.

Before rendering detail, establish:
- one dominant focal point;
- a readable overall silhouette;
- clear visual hierarchy;
- intentional directional flow;
- balanced visual weight;
- placement-compatible proportions;
- controlled negative space.

The focal point must receive the strongest contrast and highest detail.

Secondary elements must support, frame or guide attention toward the focal point.

Do not give every object equal visual importance.

Do not distribute detail evenly across the entire image.

Recommended detail hierarchy:
- highest detail at the main focal point;
- medium detail on supporting elements;
- lowest detail on framing, background and outer transitions.

The tattoo must remain readable as a small preview and from a distance.

Large shapes and silhouette are more important than micro-detail.

Use negative space deliberately.

Do not fill every empty region.

Avoid visual clutter, unnecessary filler and random decoration.

Background elements must support the tattoo structure.

Do not create a complete landscape, poster scene or cinematic environment unless the client explicitly asks for one.

Mountains, buildings, clouds, smoke, flowers and ornaments should normally function as framing or transitional tattoo elements rather than becoming a separate illustrated scene.

Preserve clear separation between overlapping elements.

Avoid accidental tangencies where edges touch awkwardly.

Avoid large dark masses merging into an unreadable shape.

Avoid weak pale compositions without sufficient contrast.

The complete artwork must feel intentionally designed for tattooing rather than adapted from generic concept art.

Respect the shape, taper, orientation and flow required by the selected body placement.

Do not draw the body placement itself.

Show only the isolated tattoo artwork.
